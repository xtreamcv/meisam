Short Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at the University of North Dakota, serving as a Senior Graduate Research Assistant at the Artificial Intelligence Research (AIR) Center. My expertise spans artificial intelligence, distributed computing, high-performance computing, and intelligent autonomous systems. I have led interdisciplinary research combining AI, HPC, and Arctic geospatial modeling, contributing to multiple Department of Defense and NSF-funded projects. I focus on designing generalized, scalable, and low-code AI workflows that allow researchers to harness HPC resources efficiently. I am also developing the first-ever global online research lab to connect talented researchers worldwide to collaborate on AI and HPC projects.
